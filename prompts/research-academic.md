## 1. Literature Review Framework

**Category:** Research Methodology
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐
**Works with:** Grok Premium | Spicy Mode

> Conduct systematic literature review on [research topic]. Structure: 1) Research question refinement (PICO format for empirical, conceptual clarity for theoretical), 2) Search strategy (databases, keywords, Boolean operators, inclusion/exclusion criteria), 3) Source identification (academic journals, books, conference proceedings - target 40-60 sources), 4) Quality assessment (methodology rigor, publication venue, citation count, bias evaluation), 5) Thematic organization (identify 5-7 major themes/debates in literature), 6) Chronological analysis (how has thinking evolved?), 7) Methodological review (approaches used, gaps), 8) Theoretical frameworks employed, 9) Contradictions and consensus areas, 10) Research gaps identification. Create: annotated bibliography template, synthesis matrix, gap analysis, future research directions.

**Why it works:** Systematic approach ensures comprehensive coverage. Thematic organization reveals patterns. Gap identification drives original contribution. These grok prompts create publication-quality reviews.

**Variations:**
- "Scoping review for emerging field exploration"
- "Meta-analysis framework with effect size calculation"
- "Systematic review following PRISMA guidelines"
- "Critical review with theoretical critique"

## 2. Research Question Development

**Category:** Research Design
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐
**Works with:** Grok Premium

> Develop robust research questions for [broad topic area]. Process: 1) Topic scoping (narrow from broad area to specific focus), 2) Literature gap analysis (what hasn't been studied?), 3) Research question types (descriptive/relational/causal - which fits?), 4) FINER criteria evaluation (Feasible, Interesting, Novel, Ethical, Relevant), 5) Question refinement (make specific, measurable, bounded), 6) Hypothesis generation (testable predictions), 7) Variable identification (independent, dependent, mediating, moderating), 8) Operational definitions, 9) Theoretical grounding (which theories inform this?), 10) Methodological implications (what methods would answer this?). Generate: 5 potential research questions with pros/cons, theoretical frameworks, and methodological approaches for each.

**Why it works:** Systematic refinement prevents vague questions. FINER criteria ensure feasibility. Theoretical grounding provides context. Grok prompts formulate answerable questions.

**Variations:**
- "Exploratory research questions for qualitative studies"
- "Hypothesis development for experimental research"
- "Mixed-methods question sequencing"
- "Applied research questions with practical implications"

## 4. Data Analysis Strategy

**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐

> Create data analysis plan for [study type] with [data description]. **Why it works:** Planned analysis prevents p-hacking. Multiple analysis types ensure robustness. Standards compliance enables publication. Grok prompts create rigorous analysis plans.

## 5. Academic Writing Structure

**Category:** Scholarly Writing
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐
**Works with:** All Grok tiers

> Outline [paper type: empirical/theoretical/review] for [journal/conference]. Structure: 1) Title (informative, searchable keywords, 10-15 words), 2) Abstract (background, objective, methods, results, conclusions - 150-250 words), 3) Introduction (hook, background, gap, research question, contribution - 3-4 pages), 4) Literature review (theoretical framework, empirical evidence, synthesis), 5) Methodology (detailed enough for replication), 6) Results (organized by research question, tables/figures), 7) Discussion (interpret findings, compare to literature, implications), 8) Limitations and future research, 9) Conclusion (key takeaways, practical implications), 10) References (30-50 for empirical). For each section: key points to cover, paragraph structure, approximate length, tone.

**Why it works:** Standard structure meets journal expectations. Section-specific guidance ensures completeness. Length guidelines prevent imbalance. These grok prompts create publishable manuscripts.

**Variations:**
- "Conference paper (shorter, presentation focus)"
- "Dissertation chapter structure"
- "Book chapter with integrative narrative"
- "Grant proposal structure"

## 9. Conference Presentation Design

**Category:** Academic Communication
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐
**Works with:** Grok Premium

> Create conference presentation for [research] in [time limit: 15/20/30 min]. Design: 1) Opening (hook, introduce self/topic, outline - 2 minutes), 2) Background and motivation (why this research matters - 3 minutes), 3) Research questions/hypotheses (clear statement - 1 minute), 4) Methods overview (enough detail to evaluate, not overwhelm - 3-4 minutes), 5) Key findings (3-5 main results with visualizations - 8-10 minutes), 6) Implications (theoretical, practical, policy - 3 minutes), 7) Limitations and future research (2 minutes), 8) Conclusion and questions (1 minute). Slide guidelines: minimal text, strong visuals, one idea per slide, readable fonts (24pt+ body, 36pt+ headings). Include: slide-by-slide script, timing notes, Q&A anticipated questions with answers.

**Why it works:** Time-boxing prevents overrun. Visual emphasis aids comprehension. Anticipated Q&A prepares defense. These grok prompts deliver impactful presentations.

**Variations:**
- "Poster presentation design and walkthrough script"
- "Lightning talk (5 minutes, high impact)"
- "Invited keynote (45-60 minutes, broader scope)"

## 10. Thesis/Dissertation Structure

**Category:** Doctoral Research
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐
**Works with:** Grok Premium | Spicy Mode

> Outline dissertation on [topic]. Structure: 1) Chapter 1 - Introduction (background, problem statement, purpose, significance, research questions, definitions, scope/limitations - 25-30 pages), 2) Chapter 2 - Literature Review (theoretical framework, empirical studies organized thematically, synthesis, gaps - 50-80 pages), 3) Chapter 3 - Methodology (paradigm, design, sample, instruments, procedures, analysis, validity, ethics - 30-40 pages), 4) Chapter 4 - Results (organized by research question, tables/figures, narrative - 40-60 pages), 5) Chapter 5 - Discussion (findings interpretation, comparison to literature, theoretical implications, practical implications, limitations, recommendations, conclusion - 35-50 pages). Total: 180-260 pages. For each chapter: detailed section headings, key arguments, approximate page counts, connection to adjacent chapters.

**Why it works:** Comprehensive outline prevents scope creep. Page estimates guide writing pace. Logical flow ensures coherence. Grok prompts structure major scholarly work.

**Variations:**
- "Three-paper dissertation (publishable articles format)"
- "Mixed-methods dissertation with integrated findings"
- "Theoretical dissertation with conceptual contributions"
- "Applied/practitioner dissertation with implementation focus"

## 15. Meta-Analysis Protocol

**Category:** Evidence Synthesis
**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐

> Design meta-analysis on [topic]. Protocol: 1) PICO question (Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome), 2) Inclusion/exclusion criteria (study design, publication type, language, date range), 3) Search strategy (databases, keywords, hand search), 4) Study selection process (screening, full-text review, consensus), 5) Data extraction (variables, outcomes, effect sizes, moderators), 6) Quality assessment (risk of bias tool selection), 7) Effect size calculation (standardized mean difference, odds ratio, correlation), 8) Heterogeneity assessment (I² statistic, Q test), 9) Analysis plan (fixed vs random effects, subgroup analyses, meta-regression), 10) Publication bias evaluation (funnel plot, Egger's test). **Why it works:** Systematic protocol ensures replicability. Bias assessment maintains quality. Statistical rigor produces reliable estimates. These grok prompts synthesize evidence quantitatively.

**Variations:**
- "Network meta-analysis (multiple treatment comparison)"
