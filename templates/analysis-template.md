## Market Analysis Template

**Description**: Conduct comprehensive market research to identify opportunities, threats, and strategic positioning.

```
Perform market analysis for [MARKET/INDUSTRY]:

**Analysis Scope**:
- Geographic focus: [REGION/COUNTRY/GLOBAL]
- Time period: [CURRENT/5-YEAR TREND/FORECAST]
- Market segment: [SPECIFIC NICHE OR BROAD CATEGORY]

**Business Context**:
- Your position: [NEW ENTRANT/EXISTING PLAYER/INVESTOR]
- Product/service: [WHAT YOU'RE ANALYZING]
- Investment capacity: [BUDGET RANGE]
- Timeline: [WHEN DECISIONS ARE NEEDED]

**Analysis Components**:
- Market size and growth rate
- Key market segments and demographics
- Customer behavior and preferences
- Pricing dynamics and trends
- Distribution channels
- Regulatory environment
- Technology disruptions

**Competitive Landscape**:
- Major players: [LIST KNOWN COMPETITORS]
- Market concentration: [FRAGMENTED/CONSOLIDATED]
- Barriers to entry: [HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW]

**Data Sources Available**: [SURVEYS/REPORTS/INTERNAL DATA]

Provide:
1. Executive summary with key findings
2. Market size and growth projections
3. Customer segmentation analysis
4. Competitive intensity assessment
5. Opportunity identification (whitespace)
6. Threat analysis (disruptions, regulations)
7. Market entry/expansion recommendations
8. Risk factors and mitigation strategies
9. Data visualization suggestions
10. Next steps for deeper research
```

**Usage Tips**: Include any existing market data or research you have access to. Specify your decision timeline - strategic planning vs. immediate action changes the analysis depth.

## SWOT Analysis Template

**Description**: Evaluate Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats for strategic planning and decision-making.

```
Conduct SWOT analysis for [COMPANY/PROJECT/PRODUCT]:

**Entity Being Analyzed**:
- Name: [ORGANIZATION/INITIATIVE]
- Industry: [SECTOR]
- Stage: [STARTUP/GROWTH/MATURE]
- Geographic scope: [LOCAL/REGIONAL/GLOBAL]

**Internal Context** (for Strengths/Weaknesses):
- Core competencies: [WHAT YOU DO WELL]
- Resources: [FINANCIAL/HUMAN/TECHNICAL]
- Current performance: [METRICS/KPIs]
- Organizational culture: [CHARACTERISTICS]
- Brand perception: [REPUTATION]

**External Context** (for Opportunities/Threats):
- Market conditions: [GROWING/STABLE/DECLINING]
- Industry trends: [KEY DEVELOPMENTS]
- Regulatory changes: [UPCOMING LEGISLATION]
- Technology shifts: [INNOVATIONS AFFECTING SECTOR]
- Economic factors: [RELEVANT MACRO TRENDS]

**Strategic Questions**:
- Goal: [WHAT YOU'RE TRYING TO ACHIEVE]
- Timeline: [PLANNING HORIZON]
- Risk tolerance: [CONSERVATIVE/MODERATE/AGGRESSIVE]

Provide:
1. **STRENGTHS** (Internal Positive):
   - Core capabilities and advantages
   - Resources and assets
   - Market position strengths

2. **WEAKNESSES** (Internal Negative):
   - Capability gaps
   - Resource constraints
   - Competitive disadvantages

3. **OPPORTUNITIES** (External Positive):
   - Market trends to leverage
   - Unmet customer needs
   - Partnership possibilities
   - Technology enablers

4. **THREATS** (External Negative):
   - Competitive pressures
   - Market headwinds
   - Regulatory challenges
   - Disruptive technologies

5. Strategy recommendations based on SWOT intersections:
   - SO: Use strengths to capture opportunities
   - WO: Address weaknesses to pursue opportunities
   - ST: Use strengths to mitigate threats
   - WT: Minimize weaknesses to avoid threats

6. Priority action items with timeline
```

**Usage Tips**: Ground your SWOT in specific, measurable factors rather than vague statements. Use this template for quarterly strategic reviews or before major decisions.

## A/B Test Results Analysis Template

**Description**: Analyze experiment results with statistical rigor to make confident, data-driven decisions.

```
Analyze A/B test results for [TEST NAME]:

**Test Setup**:
- Hypothesis: [WHAT YOU EXPECTED TO HAPPEN]
- Variants tested: [CONTROL (A) vs. VARIANT (B, C, etc.)]
- What changed: [SPECIFIC DIFFERENCES BETWEEN VARIANTS]
- Primary metric: [CONVERSION RATE/REVENUE/ENGAGEMENT/etc.]
- Secondary metrics: [SUPPORTING METRICS]

**Test Execution**:
- Sample size: [USERS PER VARIANT]
- Test duration: [START and END DATES]
- Traffic split: [50/50, 70/30, etc.]
- Randomization method: [HOW USERS WERE ASSIGNED]

**Results Data**:
- Control performance: [METRIC VALUES]
- Variant performance: [METRIC VALUES]
- Sample sizes: [ACTUAL USERS/SESSIONS]

**Test Requirements**:
- Statistical significance threshold: [TYPICALLY 95%]
- Minimum detectable effect: [SMALLEST MEANINGFUL CHANGE]
- Decision timeline: [WHEN TO SHIP OR ITERATE]

**Context**:
- User segments affected: [ALL/MOBILE/NEW USERS/etc.]
- Known confounding factors: [SEASONALITY, CONCURRENT TESTS]

Provide:
1. Statistical significance assessment (p-value, confidence intervals)
2. Practical significance evaluation (is the lift meaningful?)
3. Winner declaration or recommendation to continue testing
4. Uplift calculation (percentage improvement)
5. Impact projection (if rolled out to 100% of users)
6. Segment-level analysis (did any segments respond differently?)
7. Secondary metrics impact (any negative side effects?)
8. Sample size validation (was test properly powered?)
9. Threats to validity (selection bias, novelty effect, etc.)
10. Recommendation: Ship/Iterate/Kill
11. Lessons learned and follow-up test ideas
```

**Usage Tips**: Include your hypothesis and expected outcome to help identify surprising results. Mention any known issues during the test (bugs, traffic anomalies).
